Trouble connecting Outlook?

Most Microsoft accounts connect without any issues. However, if your account is provided by a university or employer, their IT department may have restricted which third-party apps can access it. This is a security policy on their end — not a problem with inroad.

Why university accounts are sometimes blocked

Universities manage their own Microsoft 365 tenants and can choose to require IT admin approval before any external app is granted access. When this restriction is in place, you'll either see an "Approval required" screen or the connection will fail silently. There is nothing you can do to bypass this — it requires your university IT team to whitelist the app.

The simplest fix: connect a personal Microsoft account (Outlook.com or Hotmail) instead of your university one. Your emails will still come from that address and reach recipients just as effectively.

How to connect a personal Microsoft account

1
If you don't already have one, create a free personal Microsoft account at outlook.com. Use your name — e.g. jane.smith@outlook.com — so emails look professional.
2
On your inroad dashboard, click Connect Outlook and sign in with your personal Microsoft account when prompted.
3
Once connected, clicking Send → on any match card will send the email directly from your personal Outlook address.

Still not working?

If you're using a personal account and still seeing an error, it may be a temporary issue. Try disconnecting and reconnecting from the dashboard. If the problem persists, get in touch and we'll sort it out.
